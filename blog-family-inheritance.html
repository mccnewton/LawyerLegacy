Protecting Your Family's Inheritance

Strategies to preserve and protect your legacy for future generations

Back to Resources
Protecting family wealth requires comprehensive planning strategies
June 10, 2024 | Sharon K. Lowry | 8 min read

Building wealth for your family is only half the battle. The other half is ensuring that wealth is preserved and protected for future generations. Without proper planning, even substantial inheritances can be diminished by taxes, poor decisions, or unforeseen circumstances.

Common Threats to Family Inheritance

Understanding the potential threats to your family's inheritance is the first step in developing an effective protection strategy:

Tax Erosion

Federal and state estate taxes can significantly reduce the inheritance passed to your heirs.

Creditor Claims

Lawsuits, business debts, or personal creditors can target inherited assets.

Divorce

Marital dissolution can split inherited assets with former spouses.

Poor Management

Lack of financial education or poor investment decisions can waste inheritance.

Protection Strategies

1. Trust Structures

Various trust structures can provide asset protection while maintaining family control:

  • Discretionary Trusts: Give trustees flexibility to distribute assets based on beneficiary needs
  • Spendthrift Trusts: Protect against beneficiary creditors and poor spending habits
  • Generation-Skipping Trusts: Preserve wealth across multiple generations while minimizing taxes
  • Domestic Asset Protection Trusts: Provide creditor protection in favorable jurisdictions

2. Tax Planning Strategies

Proactive tax planning can significantly increase the amount passed to your heirs:

  • Annual Gifting: Use annual exclusions to transfer wealth tax-free during lifetime
  • Charitable Strategies: Reduce estate taxes while supporting causes you care about
  • Life Insurance: Provide liquidity to pay estate taxes without selling assets
  • Valuation Discounts: Reduce taxable value through strategic entity structures
Advanced Strategy: Dynasty Trusts

Dynasty trusts can preserve wealth for multiple generations by avoiding estate taxes at each generational transfer. These long-term trusts require careful planning but can provide centuries of family wealth protection.

3. Family Governance

Establishing family governance structures helps ensure responsible stewardship of inherited wealth:

  • Family Constitutions: Document family values and expectations for wealth stewardship
  • Next-Generation Education: Prepare heirs for responsible wealth management
  • Professional Advisory Teams: Establish relationships with trusted financial advisors
  • Regular Family Meetings: Maintain communication about family financial matters

Special Considerations for Different Assets

Business Interests

Family businesses require special attention to ensure continuity and value preservation:

  • Succession planning and leadership development
  • Buy-sell agreements among family members
  • Valuation discounts for minority interests
  • Management vs. ownership structures

Real Estate

Real estate holdings need careful planning to avoid forced sales:

  • Qualified Personal Residence Trusts (QPRTs) for family homes
  • Conservation easements for large properties
  • Real estate LLCs for asset protection
  • Liquidity planning for maintenance and taxes

Retirement Accounts

Recent law changes affect how retirement accounts pass to beneficiaries:

  • Understanding the 10-year rule for inherited IRAs
  • Strategies for surviving spouses
  • Trust as IRA beneficiary considerations
  • Roth conversion strategies

Taking Action

Protecting your family's inheritance requires a comprehensive, long-term approach that addresses:

Current estate plan review and updates
Tax-efficient wealth transfer strategies
Asset protection planning
Family governance and education programs
Professional advisory team coordination
Time is Critical

The sooner you implement inheritance protection strategies, the more effective they become. Many techniques require advance planning and cannot be implemented retroactively.
